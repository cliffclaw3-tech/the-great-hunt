# The Great Hunt Field Test Checklist

Use this for real-world tests at garage sales, estate sales, thrift stores, antique shops, local classifieds, and Marketplace listings.

## Goal

Prove whether the app helps a normal person make better buy/pass/research decisions before real money is at risk.

## Before You Go

- Confirm the live phone app opens and Lookup works.
- Run one known item lookup as a smoke test.
- Bring a tape measure, small flashlight, magnet, loupe if you have one, and a way to take clear photos.
- Set a paper-test mindset first: save interesting finds even when you do not buy.

## For Each Item

1. Take or upload a photo when possible.
2. Enter the exact visible item name, model, brand, mark, or card/coin/book details.
3. Enter the seller ask.
4. Read the simple answer first: buy, research, or pass.
5. Open the proof: comps, rejected comps, field checks, and warnings.
6. Save the item to the queue.
7. Mark feedback:
   - Looks right
   - Wrong item
   - Bad comps
   - Too high
   - Too low
   - Too confusing
8. Add a note with what you saw in person.

## What Counts As A Good Result

- The app finds the right broad item.
- It warns when identifiers are missing.
- It rejects parts, accessories, replicas, reprints, and wrong-category comps.
- It says research mode instead of pretending to know.
- It gives useful next checks before you buy.

## What Counts As A Bad Result

- It prices an accessory like the full item.
- It compares a replica/reprint to an original.
- It ignores condition problems.
- It gives a confident value from one weak comp.
- It misses obvious maker/model details.
- It cannot explain why it gave the answer.

## Daily Wrap

- Save at least 10 tested finds.
- Note the weakest category of the day.
- Note any item you would have bought before using the app but passed after using it.
- Note any item the app made you take more seriously.
- Do not count profit unless there is sold-comp proof or a completed paper-trade outcome.
