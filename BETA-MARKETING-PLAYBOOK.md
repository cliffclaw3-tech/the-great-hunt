# The Great Hunt Beta Marketing Playbook

## Purpose

Get The Great Hunt into the hands of real hunters before public marketing: garage sale buyers, estate sale regulars, collectors, part-time resellers, antique booth owners, flea market sellers, and marketplace flippers.

The beta is not a vanity signup list. The goal is to recruit people who will test real items in the field, tell us when the app is wrong, and help prove whether The Great Hunt deserves a bigger launch.

## Beta Promise

Short version:

> The Great Hunt helps you check resale value before you buy, so you can spot better finds and avoid expensive guesses.

Plain-language disclaimer:

> The app gives research estimates, not guaranteed profit. It should help you decide what deserves a closer look.

## Ideal Beta Tester

Look for people who already do at least one of these:

- Go to garage sales, estate sales, auctions, flea markets, thrift stores, antique malls, or closeout aisles.
- Sell on eBay, Facebook Marketplace, Mercari, Whatnot, Etsy, or local collector groups.
- Collect sports cards, video games, comics, watches, tools, audio gear, pottery, books, knives, coins, or vintage goods.
- Have bought something before and wondered, "Did I miss a good deal?"

Avoid early testers who only want to browse the app but will not test real items.

## Beta Agreement Copy

Use this at signup before granting access:

> By joining The Great Hunt beta, I agree to keep beta access, private links, screenshots, pricing, and unreleased features confidential until public launch. I understand The Great Hunt provides research estimates, not guaranteed resale prices, appraisals, or profit. I am responsible for my own buying, selling, shipping, taxes, safety, and marketplace decisions. I agree that my feedback, saved test results, and usage notes may be used to improve the product. Beta access may be removed if the app is abused or shared outside the beta.

Checkbox label:

> I agree to keep the beta private and understand values are estimates, not guarantees.

## Beta Scout Funnel

Target:

- Find 100 possible testers.
- Invite 40.
- Onboard 15.
- Get 100 real item tests.
- Collect 10 strong field stories.

Stages:

- Prospect: likely tester found.
- Invited: personal message sent.
- Interested: replied or filled form.
- Accepted: has beta code and agreement accepted.
- Activated: completed one real lookup.
- Field tester: completed three real lookups or saved one find.
- Champion: gave useful feedback or a story worth quoting.

## Where To Find Testers

Best first channels:

- Local Facebook groups for garage sales, estate sales, thrifting, antiques, collectibles, and reselling.
- Reddit communities where beta requests are allowed.
- Flea market and antique mall vendors.
- Estate sale companies and regular shoppers.
- eBay seller groups and small reseller Discords.
- Sports card, comic, video game, tool, and vintage audio collector groups.
- Friends of friends who already buy and sell used goods.

Do not mass spam. The early brand should feel personal, useful, and invited.

## Invite Messages

### Garage Sale / Estate Sale Buyer

Hey, I am building a private beta for The Great Hunt, a phone tool that helps people check resale value before buying at garage sales, estate sales, thrift stores, and flea markets.

I am looking for a few real hunters to test it on 5 actual items this week. It is free during beta. The app gives research estimates, not guaranteed profit, and I mainly need honest feedback when it gets something wrong.

Would you want early access?

### Reseller

Hey, I am testing a private beta called The Great Hunt. It is built for quick field research: photo lookup, active listings, sold comps when available, fees, shipping traps, and buy/research/pass guidance.

I am looking for resellers who will test 5 real items and tell me where the app is wrong or too slow. Free beta access, private for now.

Interested?

### Collector

Hey, I am building The Great Hunt, a private beta tool for checking item value before buying collectibles, cards, games, books, tools, audio gear, and vintage finds.

I am looking for collectors who can test a few real items and tell me whether the research feels trustworthy. It gives estimates, not appraisals or guaranteed profit.

Would you like beta access?

### Local Vendor

Hey, I am testing a beta phone app for people who buy and sell used goods. It helps check whether an item deserves a closer look before spending money.

I would love feedback from someone who sees real inventory, not just internet examples. Free beta access if you are willing to test a few items and tell me what feels right or wrong.

## Signup Form Questions

Keep this short:

- Name
- Email
- Phone type: iPhone, Android, desktop only
- What do you hunt for?
- Where do you usually find items?
- How often do you buy or research items?
- What tools do you use now?
- Can you test 5 real items in the next 7 days?
- Are you willing to share feedback when the app is wrong?
- Beta agreement checkbox

## Tester Mission

Give each accepted tester one simple mission:

> Test 5 real items this week. Save anything interesting. Mark anything wrong, confusing, too high, or too low. We care more about honest results than perfect results.

Required asks:

- Try at least one photo lookup.
- Enter the seller asking price when known.
- Save at least one item to the queue.
- Mark feedback on at least one result.

## Follow-Up Messages

### Day 1

Thanks for joining The Great Hunt beta. Your mission is simple: test 5 real items and tell us where the app helps or gets confused.

Start here: https://thegreathunt.io

Use the beta code you were given. Please remember values are estimates, not guarantees.

### Day 3

Quick check-in: have you tested your first item yet?

The most useful beta feedback is not "it worked." It is stuff like wrong item, bad comps, too high, too low, or confusing. That is what helps us make it trustworthy.

### Day 7

Thank you for testing The Great Hunt. If you only have time for one thing, send us your most interesting item and whether the app made you buy, pass, or research more.

## Beta Success Metrics

Minimum signal before public launch:

- 100 real item tests
- 25 photo lookups
- 20 saved finds
- 10 clear feedback marks
- 10 testers who completed at least one lookup
- 5 testers who completed three or more lookups
- 3 field stories that can become marketing examples

Trust threshold:

- 90% of tested items return a useful answer:
  - sold comps found
  - active comparable listings found
  - barcode/product match found
  - or a clear "not enough evidence" explanation

Do not count vague answers as wins. "Cannot prove sold value, but here are comparable asking prices" is useful. Pretending to know is not.

## Content Ideas For Soft Marketing

Use these after testers create real examples:

- "Garage sale find: buy, pass, or research?"
- "Active listings are not sold comps."
- "The app said research, not buy. Here is why."
- "Shipping killed the deal."
- "Wrong item warning: accessory vs full product."
- "One photo, three checks before spending cash."
- "What would you hunt for this weekend?"

Avoid income claims until there is real proof.

## Public Language To Use

Good:

- "Check resale value before you buy."
- "Find sold comps when available."
- "See active asking prices when sold data is thin."
- "Know when there is not enough evidence."
- "Save finds and review them later."

Avoid:

- "Guaranteed profit."
- "Never miss a deal."
- "Instant appraisal."
- "Always accurate."
- "Make $5,000 this month."

## 14-Day Beta Recruiting Sprint

Day 1:

- Create signup form.
- Add beta agreement checkbox.
- Write first 30 prospects into the tracking sheet.

Day 2:

- Invite 10 likely testers.
- Post one soft request in a local group where allowed.

Day 3:

- Follow up with replies.
- Invite 10 more.

Day 4:

- Review first usage.
- Fix any obvious onboarding confusion.

Day 5:

- Invite 10 more.
- Ask each active tester to test one weekend item.

Days 6-7:

- Weekend field push.
- Collect screenshots, notes, and saved finds.

Day 8:

- Review what failed.
- Identify weakest categories.

Days 9-11:

- Invite 10-20 category-specific testers based on weaknesses.

Day 12:

- Ask active testers for one quote or field story.

Day 13:

- Decide whether the beta is producing useful evidence.

Day 14:

- Summarize:
  - strongest use case
  - weakest category
  - best story
  - biggest trust problem
  - next product fix
